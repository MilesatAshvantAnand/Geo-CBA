What are Hurricanes and how do they form
Evaporation: Warm ocean water evaporates into the atmosphere, forming clouds.
Condensation : As the clouds rise, they cool, and the water vapor condenses into tiny water droplets.
Release of latent heat: As the water vapor condenses, it releases latent heat, which warms the surrounding air. This warm air rises even higher and cools again, repeating the cycle.
Formation of thunderstorms: The rising warm air creates a circulation pattern that draws more air into the storm. This can lead to the formation of thunderstorms, which produce even more latent heat.
Development of a low-pressure centre: As the storm intensifies, a low-pressure centre develops at its centre. This low pressure attracts more air, which spirals inward and forms the characteristic hurricane winds.
Formation of the eye: At the centre of a mature hurricane, there is an area of calm winds called the eye. The eye is surrounded by the eyewall, which is the area of the hurricane with the strongest winds.
